Bio

Nathan Neeley is currently a second-year M.M. student and teaching assistant in music theory at the University of Cincinnati's College-Conservatory of Music. He received his B.A. in music from Florida State University (magna cum laude, Pi Kappa Lambda) in 2016. His research interests include Stravinsky, Ives, 20th-century music, post-tonal theory, transformational theory, film music, and popular music. He has presented his research at Bowling Green State University (2018), the Society for Music Analysis (TAGS) in Durham, England (2018), the University of Cincinnati-CCM (2018), and the Forum for the International Association for Word and Music Studies in Düsseldorf, Germany (2018), and he will present at Music and the Moving Image in May 2019. Outside of music, he enjoys computer programming, tennis, and watching TV shows and movies.
